BFSI and Healthcare as candidates. Regulated, legacy-heavy, high stakes for agent errors.

Land & Expand
How we enter accounts, deepen coverage, and make removal expensive
Land: one workflow, fast proof
Entry motion
Single scoped workflow. Not a platform sale.

Prove ROI in 30-60 days. FDE makes this happen reliably.

Goal of the land
A referenceable proof point inside the account

Not just revenue. An internal champion with evidence. The seed for expansion.

What has the typical land workflow looked like in closed deals? Is there a pattern in which workflow type lands fastest and creates the strongest internal champion?
Expand: adjacent systems, growing ACV
The motion
FDE maps the environment. Expansion is a continuation, not a new sale.

ACV moves from $20K to $200K+ as system coverage deepens.

The metric
Systems per account

Primary expansion health signal. More systems touched means deeper lock-in and higher lifetime value.

Has expansion happened organically in existing accounts, or has it required a new sales motion? And who initiates it, us or the customer?
Switching cost · Commercial arc
Retention by architecture
Depth of coverage creates operational dependency

Not contractual stickiness. The more workflows Adopt governs, the more disruptive removal becomes.

Commercial arc
Land $20-50K. Expand to $200K+.

Lifetime value per account is multiples of ACV if expansion is systematic, not ad hoc.

The GTM job
Make expansion repeatable

Not dependent on FDE initiative or founder relationships. A system, not a hustle.

Is there a deliberate expansion playbook today, or is growth in existing accounts largely ad hoc?

Who
Large enough. Small enough. Fast enough.

Large enough to have enterprise relationships. Small enough to need us for differentiation. Moves fast and is agile. Examples: Hexaware, Mphasis, Persistent Systems, Birlasoft, boutique RPA-to-AI transition firms.

Tier 1 is high on all three. If they're dabbling in agentic AI rather than building a practice, deprioritize.

Big 4 move slowly and extract heavily. Case study library from Tier 1 makes that conversation worth having.

Has any SI relationship started organically through an existing customer or deployment?
Acquisition motion
The principle
Don't lead with a partnership pitch

Lead through a shared customer problem. Let them see Adopt work in a live environment first.

The funnel
Customer ref · co-deploy · case study · formalize · escalate up

Each step earns the next. Formalization becomes easy after they have skin in the game.

Incentive structure · Enablement
Revenue share
15-20% referral on closed deals

Baseline. Not the primary motivator for most SIs.

Real incentive
Services margin on Adopt deployments

A $500K services engagement on top of a $200K Adopt contract is the number that changes partner behavior.

Enablement stack
Certification · playbooks · sandbox · partner success

Makes partners self-sufficient. FDEs train partner technical teams. Active partners, not nominal ones.

How does intelligence from partner deployments flow back to the platform? A solid data capture strategy is going to be critical.
